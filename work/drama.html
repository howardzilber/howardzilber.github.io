About Me

When I was young I loved reading novels. So when I started thinking about writing imaginative literature, I assumed it would be fiction. But my first attempts weren’t very promising.
I happened to come across a book by Walter Kerr about the theatre. He emphasized the mythic origins of drama, the way that at its best theatre is larger than life, and incredibly intense. I was hooked. I wrote a first overly ambitious play, which somehow combined Oedipus Rex and the Holocaust! But I persisted and got better. Soon elements of verse showed up. This puzzled me. But then I started singing those parts. That’s how I came to write musicals. The thing about my work that’s quite unusual is how varied it is in tone and subject matter. The Pied Piper is a sweet, lighthearted musical. Saul is a tragedy. The Odyssey is soaring and romantic. The Unwitnessing of a Murder is as dark as a black hole. And there are many others, each with it’s specific world. All I can say is life is similarly varied. Imagine all the things happening at this very moment. While lovers are falling in love, others are being tortured. While bombs fall from the sky, babies are being born.
That is life. That is art.
